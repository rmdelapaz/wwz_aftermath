üéØ Identification

  • Massive size with riot gear armor
  • Helmet with visor (can be broken)
  • Loud metallic footsteps when approaching
  • Distinctive roar before charging
üîä Audio Cue: Deep growl followed by loud roar, heavy metallic stomping. You'll hear a Bull before you see it.

‚öôÔ∏è Mechanics & Behavior

  • Charge Attack: Runs in straight line, grabs and slams first target
  • Wind-up Time: 2-3 seconds before charge begins
  • Charge Distance: Approximately 30 meters
  • Armor: Front armor reduces damage by 50%
  • Visor: Can be broken to expose head for critical damage
  • Post-Charge: Brief recovery period after charge

üíö Weaknesses

  • Back is unarmored - Full damage from behind
  • Visor breaks - Concentrated fire exposes head
  • Can't turn while charging - Sidestep to avoid
  • Explosives bypass armor - C4, grenades, RPGs work
  • Stunnable during wind-up - Stun gun, flashbang stop charge

üéÆ Universal Counter Strategies

  1. Listen for audio cues - React before visual contact
  2. Focus fire the visor - Team should concentrate shots
  3. Sidestep at last second - Roll perpendicular to charge
  4. Use environment - Bulls hit walls and get stunned
  5. Save heavy weapons - RPG = instant kill
  6. Explosive placement - Pre-place C4 or claymores in paths
üí° Pro Tip: Bulls always charge the closest target. Use this to bait charges into walls or away from vulnerable teammates. A Bull hitting a wall is stunned for 3 seconds!

  3. Headshot for instant kill - Don't risk body shots
  4. Call out location - "Screamer back left!"

üí° Pro Tip: Screamers spawn at specific points on each map. Learn these locations and pre-check them. On higher difficulties, always have one player watching the team's back.

  3. Clear the area first - Kill nearby zombies before engaging
  4. Watch for multiples - Gasbags often spawn in pairs
  5. Have escape route - Always know where to run
üí° Pro Tip: You can use Gasbag explosions tactically! Shoot the body when surrounded by regular zombies (but far from team) to clear crowds.

üí° Universal Special Zombie Tips

Audio is Everything

Every special has unique audio cues. Play with good headphones and learn these sounds - you'll often hear specials before seeing them, giving crucial reaction time.

Spawn Patterns

  • Specials spawn at set locations on each map - learn them
  • Difficulty affects spawn frequency, not locations
  • Specials often spawn in response to noise or progress triggers
  • Multiple specials can spawn simultaneously on higher difficulties

Communication

Always call out specials with direction: "Bull behind us!" or "Screamer on the left!" Quick, clear communication saves lives.

Difficulty Scaling

  • Easy/Normal: Specials spawn alone, lower health
  • Hard: Pairs of specials, standard health
  • Insane: Groups of specials, increased health
  • Extreme: Multiple special types together, maximum health
