üí° Document Hunting Tips

  • Explore Thoroughly: Documents are often hidden in side rooms, offices, and areas off the main path
  • Check Common Spots: Look on desks, shelves, filing cabinets, and near computers
  • Before Defense Sections: Many documents are placed before major defense encounters - explore before triggering them
  • Listen for Audio Cues: Documents sometimes emit a subtle paper rustling sound when nearby
  • Play on Lower Difficulties: Use Easy or Normal difficulty for document hunting to avoid zombie pressure
  • Use Slasher Class: The increased movement speed helps with exploration
  • Co-op Strategy: Have teammates hold positions while one player explores
  • Multiple Playthroughs: Don't worry about getting everything in one run - documents save across playthroughs

üìñ Document Types & Lore

Documents in World War Z: Aftermath come in various forms, each providing unique insights into the apocalypse:

  • Military Reports: Official communications detailing military operations and evacuation protocols
  • Personal Diaries: Intimate accounts from survivors describing their experiences
  • Scientific Research: Medical and scientific findings about the virus and potential cures
  • News Articles: Media coverage documenting the outbreak's progression
  • Government Communications: Official statements and emergency broadcasts
  • Survivor Notes: Messages left behind by those trying to survive
  • Corporate Documents: Business records showing how corporations responded to the crisis
  • Historical Records: Documents placing the outbreak in historical context
