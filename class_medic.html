üíâ Core Ability

Stim Pistol

Effect: Heals teammates for 25% health plus temporary health. Can also self-heal.

Charges: Starts with 4 charges (can be increased with perks)

Range: Medium range, no damage falloff

Cooldown: Regenerates 1 charge every 90 seconds

Pro Tip: The stim pistol has surprisingly long range - you can heal from across most rooms!

üéØ Medic Skill Tree

How the Skill System Works:
  • Progress through levels 1-30 to unlock new perk segments
  • Choose ONE perk per segment to activate
  • Red perks are special alternatives that cost more (red coins)
  • You can buy multiple perks in each segment but only use one at a time
  • Perks cost 150-500 yellow coins, red perks cost 150-500 red coins
  • Level 10, 20, and 30 perks are automatic and always active

‚≠ê Prestige Rank Perks

About Prestige Perks:
  • Unlocked by reaching Prestige Ranks 1-9
  • These are PASSIVE bonuses that are always active once unlocked
  • Stack with your regular perks for even more power
  • Each costs 7500 yellow coins to unlock
  • Persist through prestige resets

Prestige Progression

Rank 1: Painkiller II
Stim Pistol effects last 25% longer.
Extends the duration of all Stim Pistol benefits, giving more value per shot.
Rank 2: Triage II
Apply Medkits 25% faster (base: 4 seconds).
Stacks with the regular Triage perk for ultra-fast healing.
Rank 3: Remote Healthcare
Stim Pistol range increased by 25%.
Heal teammates from safer distances - crucial for higher difficulties.
Rank 4: Navy Seal II
SMG range increased by 25%.
Makes SMGs more versatile for medium-range combat.
Rank 6: Empathy II
Using the Stim Pistol on a teammate will also apply 10% of its effect to you.
Passive self-healing that stacks with the regular Empathy perk.
Rank 7: Remote Healthcare II
Stim Pistol range increased by 10%.
Stacks with Remote Healthcare for 35% total range increase.
Rank 8: Hypervitaminosis
After using a Medkit, you will apply 10% of its effect to a random ally.
Free healing spread - every Medkit helps multiple teammates.
Rank 9: Paramedic II
Revive speed increased by 10%.
The final prestige perk - stacks with regular Paramedic for 60% faster revives.
üí° Prestige Strategy: Focus on reaching at least Prestige Rank 3 for the essential healing improvements. Remote Healthcare is particularly valuable as it significantly improves your ability to heal from safe positions. The later ranks are nice bonuses but less critical.

The rounds of the Stim Pistol are replaced with healing gas syringes that burst upon contact with surfaces, releasing healing gas that grants a 75% temporary health boost to all players.
Completely changes how the Stim Pistol works - creates area healing instead of targeted.

üéØ Recommended Builds

üåü Beginner Build - "Stay Alive"

Focus on survival and basic healing. Perfect for learning the class.

Essential Perks:
  • Level 1-3: Doctor in the House (Medkits heal 10% more)
  • Level 4-6: Paramedic (50% faster revive)
  • Level 7-9: Painkiller (25% more temp health, lasts 50% longer)
  • Level 11-13: Wheatgrass (Health increased by 25%)
  • Level 14-16: Efficiency (30% chance Medkit not consumed)
üí° Tip: This build maximizes your survival and gives you room to make mistakes while learning.
Best Prestige Perks: Triage II (Rank 2) for faster healing, Painkiller II (Rank 1) for longer buffs.

‚öîÔ∏è Combat Medic Build - "Battle Healer"

Aggressive healing through combat. Heal by killing.

Essential Perks:
  • Level 14-16: Combat Medic (Kill 15 zombies for Medkit chance)
  • Level 17-19: Pickpocket (Kill 15 zombies to refill equipment)
  • Level 21-23: In the Zone (50% damage boost after healing)
  • Level 24-26: Battle Surgeon (Stim heals 10% health)
  • Level 27-29: Lobotomy (2 rounds per SMG headshot)
üí° Tip: Stay aggressive - your kills generate healing resources and boost team damage!
Best Prestige Perks: Navy Seal II (Rank 4) for better SMG range, Empathy II (Rank 6) for self-healing.

üõ°Ô∏è Support Build - "Guardian Angel"

Maximum team support and protection. For coordinated teams.

Essential Perks:
  • Level 1-3 Red: Breathe Deeply (Healing gas clouds)
  • Level 4-6: Pick Me Up (Revive gives temp health)
  • Level 14-16: Good Karma (Medkit heals you too)
  • Level 21-23: Forgive and Forget (25% less friendly fire)
  • Level 24-26: Secret Ingredient (Stim grants masking)
üí° Tip: Stay back, focus on healing and reviving. Your buffs keep the team alive.
Best Prestige Perks: Remote Healthcare I & II (Ranks 3 & 7) for maximum healing range, Paramedic II (Rank 9) for fastest revives.

üìç Situational Tactics

üåä Swarm Defense

  1. Pre-stim all teammates before swarm
  2. Apply Fighting Fit damage buff
  3. Position yourself safely in back
  4. Use Combat Medic for passive healing
  5. Save stims for emergency revives

üèÉ Running Sections

  1. Stay in middle of group
  2. Heal runners as they move
  3. Drop medkits at chokepoints
  4. Use Painkiller for damage immunity
  5. Quick revive anyone who falls

‚ö†Ô∏è Common Medic Mistakes to Avoid

  • Hoarding stims: Use them proactively, they regenerate!
  • Tunnel vision: Don't forget to shoot while healing
  • Bad positioning: Stay back but not too far
  • Ignoring temp health: It's just as important as real health
  • Wrong perk choices: Combat Medic is essential
  • Forgetting Fighting Fit: The damage buff is huge

üåü Tips for New Medic Players

  1. Stim early and often - Don't wait for critical health
  2. Learn stim range - It's longer than you think
  3. Watch everyone's health - Not just lowest player
  4. Communicate healing - Tell team when buffing
  5. Position smartly - Middle of team, near cover
  6. Use Combat Medic - Free healing from kills
  7. Master Fighting Fit timing - Before big fights
  8. Don't forget your gun - You need to kill too
