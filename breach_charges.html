Understanding breach charges and special door mechanics in World War Z: Aftermath

Complete guide for breach charge usage and door locations

üìä Understanding Breach Charges

What are Breach Charges?

Breach charges are special equipment items used exclusively to open breach doors.

  • Primary Purpose: Opening special reinforced doors marked with yellow paint/indicators
  • Cannot be used as weapons - they only work on designated breach doors
  • Single Use: Each charge opens one breach door
  • Team Shared: Any player can pick up and use breach charges
  • Auto-Pickup: Walk over them to automatically collect (if not carrying one)
  • One at a Time: Can only carry one breach charge per player
Door Types: Required - Must be opened to progress Optional - Contains loot/shortcuts Shortcut - Alternative path

üéØ How to Use Breach Charges

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Find a Breach Charge: Look for red explosive charges on the ground or equipment racks
  2. Pick it Up: Walk over it to automatically collect (shows in equipment slot)
  3. Locate Breach Door: Look for reinforced doors with yellow markings
  4. Approach Door: Get close to the breach door
  5. Plant Charge: Hold the interact button (X/Square) when prompted
  6. Stand Back: Move away from the door after planting
  7. Door Opens: After a short delay, the door explodes open

Visual Indicators

  • Yellow Paint: Breach doors have distinctive yellow markings around them
  • Reinforced Look: Heavy metal doors that can't be opened normally
  • Prompt Appears: "Hold [X] to place breach charge" when you're close enough
  • Red Light: Some breach doors have red indicator lights
  • Equipment Icon: Breach charge appears in your equipment slot when carried

üí° Breach Charge Mechanics

Important Notes:

  • Not All Doors Need Charges: Most doors open normally or automatically
  • Story Progression: Some breach doors are mandatory for mission completion
  • Loot Rooms: Optional breach doors often contain valuable supplies
  • Team Coordination: Communicate who's carrying charges to avoid confusion
  • Respawn Logic: Breach charges don't respawn - once used, they're gone
  • No Damage: Despite the explosion animation, breach charges don't damage enemies
  • Can't Store: You can't save breach charges between missions

üí° Tips for Breach Charges

  • Communication: Call out when you find breach charges and coordinate who carries them
  • Priority: Save charges for optional loot rooms unless required for progression
  • Team Benefit: Optional breach doors often contain supplies for the whole team
  • Visual Cues: Look for yellow paint or markings to identify breach doors
  • Don't Waste: Breach charges are limited - use them wisely
  • Check Corners: Breach charges often spawn in side rooms or corners
  • Listen: Breach doors make a distinctive sound when you're near them
  • No Combat Use: Remember - these are ONLY for doors, not for fighting zombies
